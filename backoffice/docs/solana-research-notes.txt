The "Catch-Up" Strategy (2026 Standard)
When your monitor "wakes up" after a long death, it should perform these three steps in order:

1. The Immediate Resume (gRPC fromSlot)
Yellowstone gRPC supports a parameter called fromSlot. In your database, you should always be saving the Last Processed Slot ID.

When you restart, you send: SubscribeRequest(fromSlot: LAST_SAVED_SLOT).

If it’s been < 20 minutes: The stream will instantly "fast-forward" and send you every transaction you missed.

If it’s been > 20 minutes: The gRPC node will give you an error saying "Slot too old."

2. The Big Gap Recovery (RPC getTransactionsForAddress)
Since your 6-hour gap is too big for the gRPC memory, you switch to the standard RPC API to "backfill" the missing data.
In 2026, there is a specialized method called getTransactionsForAddress (introduced by Helius and adopted by others) that is 10x faster than the old way of searching.

You loop through your "active" addresses.

You ask the RPC: "Give me all transactions for this address between Slot [Died] and Slot [Now]."

Once you have processed those, your database is "synced."

3. The Live Handover
Once the backfill is done, you start the gRPC stream normally at the "Current Tip" of the blockchain to stay real-time.

How to manage "Double Data"
If both processes are alive, they will both see the same $1,000 credit at the same time. You don't want to credit your user twice in your database. You handle this with Idempotency:

The Unique Key: Every Solana transaction has a Signature (a long string of characters).

The Database Constraint: In your SQL database, set the transaction_signature column to UNIQUE.

The Logic: Both processes try to "Insert" the credit.

Process A succeeds.

Process B gets a "Duplicate Key" error from the database and simply ignores it.
